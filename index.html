  • This compares one drive with one flight - not a round trip.
  • Plane risk is a flat per-flight rate from US airline data (2013-2024, cargo and charter flights included). A short hop and a cross-country flight count the same.
  • Car risk is the average per-mile rate for US passenger vehicles (2023). Your actual route, weather, and driving aren't factored in.
  • "Fatal" means the accident killed someone - not necessarily you. Most of the fatal plane accidents in this data killed no passengers at all (only crew or ground workers), so if anything, flying is even safer than shown.
  • The car side counts deaths while the plane side counts accidents, which slightly overstates the car risk too.
Sources: NHTSA (2023 passenger vehicle occupant fatalities), FHWA (2023 vehicle miles traveled), BTS/NTSB (2013-2024 US air carrier accidents)
